# Test Prompts — 5 Scenes Per Template

Copy-paste these into the ControlBar. Each prompt is designed to generate
a **5-scene story in one shot** (the scene count is hardcoded to 1, so
paste each prompt one at a time to build up 5 scenes).

---

## Storybook (baseline — no overlays)

1. A lonely lighthouse keeper named Elara finds a glass bottle washed ashore containing a glowing map that only appears under moonlight
2. Following the map, Elara rows through a fog bank and discovers a hidden island where trees grow upside down and rivers flow into the sky
3. On the island she meets a talking fox named Sable who warns her that the island is slowly vanishing — one tree disappears each night
4. Together Elara and Sable climb the tallest inverted tree and find an ancient clock frozen at midnight, its gears clogged with black sand
5. Elara clears the sand and restarts the clock — the island erupts in golden light, the trees root back into the ground, and Sable reveals he was the island's lost guardian all along

## Comic Book (visual narrative — yellow narration boxes, white dialog bubbles)

1. CRASH! A meteor slams into downtown Apex City and retired hero Volt staggers out of his apartment to see a familiar silhouette standing in the crater — his old nemesis Obsidian has returned
2. Obsidian hurls a bus at fleeing civilians. Volt catches it mid-air, electricity crackling from his fists, and shouts "You should have stayed in that prison, Obsidian!"
3. The two clash on the rooftop of Apex Tower — Obsidian reveals he escaped because a darker force is coming and he needs Volt's help to stop it
4. Deep beneath the city they discover a massive alien machine powering up. Obsidian says "This is what broke me out. It's a beacon — and something heard it."
5. Volt and Obsidian combine their powers — lightning and shadow merge into a single blast that destroys the beacon. In the aftermath they stand side by side staring at the sky, uneasy allies

## Webtoon (visual narrative — lavender narration, soft dialog)

1. Monday morning. Mina rushes into Blossom Brew only to find the espresso machine broken again. Across the street at rival cafe Drip Drop, Jae smirks through the window
2. That evening they both reach for the last bag of high-grade Honduran beans at the specialty store. Their hands touch. Awkward silence. "You take it," they both say at the same time
3. A rainstorm traps them under the same bus shelter. Jae offers Mina his umbrella. She refuses. They argue about latte art technique until the rain stops and both are smiling
4. Mina finds a handwritten note tucked inside a coffee sleeve at her shop — "Your rosetta is actually pretty good. — J" She hides a grin from her coworker
5. On a quiet Sunday both cafes are closed. They run into each other at the park, no aprons, no rivalry. Jae says "Truce?" Mina holds up two takeaway cups — she already made him one

## Manga (visual narrative — bold narration, clean dialog)

1. Transfer student Ren Takahashi walks through the gates of Kurogane Academy. The cherry blossoms feel wrong — they're falling upward. A girl with fox ears watches from the rooftop
2. In the basement library Ren discovers an ancient scroll. The moment he touches it, spirit energy explodes outward and ghostly samurai materialize in the hallway
3. The fox-eared girl Kitsune appears and slices through the spirits with a glowing blade. "You woke them up, new kid. Now you help me put them back to sleep."
4. That night they descend into the sealed chamber beneath the school. Hundreds of yokai are stirring. The old barrier is cracking and Ren's touch seems to be the key to resealing it
5. Ren places both hands on the barrier stone. His eyes glow blue. The yokai howl and retreat. The barrier holds — for now. Kitsune says "Welcome to the real Kurogane Academy."

## Hero Quest (no overlays — epic adventure)

1. You wake at the edge of a scorched battlefield. Your sword is shattered, your armor cracked, and in the distance a dragon circles a burning castle — the kingdom of Valdris is falling
2. In the forest ruins you find the legendary blacksmith Korrin who agrees to reforge your blade using a shard of dragon fire. You must retrieve it from the dragon's own lair
3. You infiltrate the dragon's cavern through an underwater tunnel. Inside, molten gold rivers light the darkness and the dragon sleeps on a mountain of bones
4. You pry a glowing ember from the dragon's scales. It wakes. A deafening roar shakes the mountain. You sprint through collapsing tunnels as fire erupts behind you
5. Korrin forges the Emberblade in a single night. At dawn you face the dragon above Valdris. One strike of the blazing sword splits the sky — the dragon falls and the kingdom is saved

## Novel (no overlays — long-form prose)

1. Professor Lena Hargrove receives a letter from a colleague who vanished three years ago. Inside is a single photograph of a door that shouldn't exist — in the basement of her own university
2. Behind the door she finds a chamber filled with clocks running at different speeds. One clock shows a date two hundred years in the future. Another shows yesterday on repeat
3. She steps through the fastest clock and emerges in a version of the campus where the buildings are covered in vines and the sky is permanently twilight. A man who looks exactly like her missing colleague approaches
4. He explains: the door connects to branching timelines. He's been trapped, unable to find the original. Each clock leads somewhere different and several versions of him are lost across time
5. Lena builds a resonance device from parts across three timelines. She activates it in the chamber — the clocks synchronize, the branches collapse, and her colleague tumbles through, aged but alive

## Diary (no overlays — first-person journal)

1. March 3. Arrived in Kyoto. The ryokan smells like cedar and rain. I don't know why I came except that I needed to be somewhere no one knows my name
2. March 5. Wandered into a temple garden. An old woman was sweeping leaves that weren't there. She looked at me and said "You carry too many stones." I wanted to ask what she meant but she was gone
3. March 8. Found a tiny ramen shop with six seats. The chef didn't speak English, I don't speak Japanese. We communicated through pointing and laughing. Best meal of my life
4. March 12. Climbed Fushimi Inari at sunrise. Ten thousand orange gates leading up the mountain. Somewhere near the top I started crying and I don't know why. It felt like permission
5. March 15. Last day. The ryokan owner gave me a small wooden box as a gift. Inside was a smooth river stone. She said "Now you only carry one." I think I understand

## Poetry (no overlays — illustrated verse)

1. Morning arrives in watercolor — amber bleeds across the ceiling, the curtain breathes, and somewhere below a sparrow rehearses its one perfect song
2. The coffee cup holds a small ocean. Steam rises like a thought not yet spoken. I wrap both hands around it and pretend it is the warmth of someone staying
3. Rain taps the window in Morse code — a message I almost understand. The street below becomes a mirror. Everyone walks on sky
4. Evening folds the city into velvet. Streetlights bloom like paper lanterns. A stranger hums a melody I know but cannot name
5. Midnight. The room is still. I write these words on the back of silence and leave them where the morning light will find them

## Photo Journal (no overlays — documentary)

1. 6:14 AM. The fish market opens before the city wakes. A weathered hand sorts silver mackerel under fluorescent light. Forty years at the same stall, same knife, same dawn
2. The barbershop on 4th street hasn't changed its sign since 1967. Inside, Mr. Torres clips hair and trades stories. The mirror reflects three generations of the same neighborhood
3. A mural covers the entire side of the old textile factory. Local artist Priya spent four months painting it — a hundred faces from the neighborhood, each one a real person, each one asked permission
4. Friday night at the community center. Folding chairs, paper plates, somebody's abuela's rice and beans. A kid breakdances on cardboard while elders clap on beat
5. Sunday morning. The block is quiet. A cat stretches on a fire escape. Laundry hangs between buildings like prayer flags. The city breathes. I press the shutter one last time
